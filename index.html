The Pitch

Fantasy baseball is exhausting. Managing lineups for 162 games burns people out by June. Bush League is different: you make just 8 strategic decisions across the entire season, but each one matters.

Pick your powerhouse teams wisely, hunt for value in the basement tiers, and watch the drama unfold as everyone's options narrow toward the finish.

Think of it as a season-long poker game where you're betting on MLB teams instead of cards — and once you play a team, it's burned forever.

How It Works

Each quarter, you build a 4-team roster with exactly one team from each performance tier. You manually select your Tier 1 (Elite) and Tier 2 (Contender) teams, while Tier 3 (Average) and Tier 4 (Basement) teams are randomly assigned. Your score is the combined winning percentage of your four teams.

The twist? Once you manually pick a team, you can never pick them again. By Q4, your elite options are depleted, and suddenly that Tier 4 random assignment could make or break your season.

Selection Rules

Manual Selection (Tier 1 & Tier 2)

  • You choose one team from Tier 1 and one team from Tier 2 each quarter
  • The Burn Rule: Once you manually select a team, you cannot manually select them again for the rest of the season, regardless of tier changes
  • Multiple players may select the same team — there are no exclusive picks

Random Assignment (Tier 3 & Tier 4)

The application randomly assigns you one team from Tier 3 and one from Tier 4. Here's how the random pool works:

  1. Same-Quarter Protection: You cannot be randomly assigned a team you manually selected that same quarter (no duplicate teams on one roster)
  2. Fresh Slate Each Quarter: You CAN be randomly assigned a team you had in a previous quarter (whether from manual selection or prior random assignment)
  3. No Exclusivity: Multiple players may be randomly assigned the same team in the same quarter
Why this design? Keeping the random pools open prevents them from shrinking to unworkable sizes by Q4. The strategic lever is your manual burn — if you want to guarantee you never have a team again, spend a manual pick on them.

Order of Operations

Each quarter follows this sequence:

  1. Tiers Published: Commissioner re-evaluates and publishes the updated tier list based on current standings
  2. Manual Selections (48-Hour Window): Players submit their Tier 1 and Tier 2 picks (blind/simultaneous — you cannot see others' picks)
  3. Random Assignment: Application randomly assigns Tier 3 and Tier 4 teams to each player
  4. Rosters Revealed: All rosters are published and the quarter begins
  5. Scoring: Win percentages accumulate throughout the quarter

Scoring

  • Quarterly Score: Combined winning percentage of your 4 rostered teams during that quarter
  • Season Score: Cumulative winning percentage across all four quarters
  • Tie-Breaker: Average runs scored per game across all rostered teams for the period in question

Strategic Considerations

The Burn Dilemma

Do you use the Dodgers in Q1 when they're likely dominant, or save them for Q4 when your options are thin? Front-loading powerhouses gives you early wins but leaves you vulnerable late.

Tier Volatility

Teams shift tiers quarterly. A hot start from a Tier 3 team could push them to Tier 2 by Q2 — if you didn't burn them already, you can grab them as a "value" pick.

The Random Factor

You can't control Tier 3 and 4 assignments, but they become increasingly important as elite options dwindle. Embrace the chaos.

Q4 Crunch

By the final quarter, you've burned 3 elite teams and 3 contenders. With fewer games, every win matters more. This is where seasons are won and lost.
